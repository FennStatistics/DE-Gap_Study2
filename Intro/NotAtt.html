This is a decision-making game. You will play a game for {{num_rounds}} rounds.

In each round, you will choose between two options: Option A or Option B. In the first five rounds, you will not learn about the outcomes of your choices. After the fifth round, you will learn about the outcomes of both options: the option you chose and the option you did not choose. Your choices will impact your bonus payment and the environment.

Outcomes affect the Environment:

Your choices have environmental consequences. Some options cause carbon (CO2) emissions in the real world. Prior to the study, we purchased CO2 emission certificates. There is only a limited number of certificates on the market, and they regulate how much CO2 can be emitted in total. Think of a CO2 certificate as an allowance to emit a certain amount of CO2. If you choose an option that causes CO2 emissions, we will return the corresponding amount of CO2 certificates, allowing the buyer to emit this amount of CO2. If you choose an option without CO2 emissions, we will calculate how much CO2 you saved compared to the other option. We will then destroy carbon certificates corresponding to your CO2 savings. Once the CO2 certificate is destroyed and taken off the market, it reduces the total amount of emission allowances that can be purchased. With less certificates on the market, less carbon can be emitted in total. This means, carbon emissions will actually be realized in the real world following your choices.

Outcomes affect your bonus payment:

In each round, you can gain or loose points.

At the end of the study, one round will be randomly drawn. The outcome from this round will decide your bonus payment and the environmental outcome. The carbon emissions of your choice in that round will then be realized. The points you gained or lost in that round will be converted into British Pounds (conversion rate: 20points ≙ £1) and will be added to your initial bonus payment of £1. Note that your bonus payment is never negative. The minimum bonus payment you can get is £0.

Example: Imagine you played 2 rounds. In round 1, you lost 10 points and the carbon emissions were 20kg, in round 2 you gained 5 pointsand 0kg of carbon emissions. At the end, the computer will draw randomly either round 1 or round 2. If round 1 is drawn, your payoff will be - 10 points, divided by 20 = - £0.5. Your bonus payment will thus be £1 (initial endowment) - £0.5 = £0.5 (around USD $0.62). The carbon emissions of 20kg will be realized by selling a CO2 certificate that allows the buyer to emit 20kg of CO2.
